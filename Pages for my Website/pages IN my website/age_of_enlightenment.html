The Age of Enlightenment: Facts,Life In That Period and More:

The Age of Enlightenment was a time period of great intellectual & philosophical movement between early 16th to early 19th Century. It is also known as the "Age of Reason". The Enlightenment scholars valued the knowledge gained by inquiry and sensory experience, also known as "Empiricism".It also gave birth to many social & political ideals like equality, tolerance and freedom of expression.

The Age of Enlightenment commenced and overlapped with the end of The Scientific Revolution. Many influential scholars and philosophers (also called as "philosophe" in French) like Voltaire, Montesqiueu, Sir Francis Bacon and Rene Descartes, with their treatises on philosophy, politics and religion spread the ideas of freedom, tolerance and supported the questioning of established beliefs and investigation of things and events i.e phenomena to gain knowledge. Philosophers like Immanuel Kant further fueled this by stating in one of his works, "Sapere Aude"- Dare to know.
